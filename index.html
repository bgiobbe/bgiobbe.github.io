Hi, my name is Beth Giobbe.

I am in the Boston Metrowest area, seeking a mid-level Software Engineer position.

I started learning computer programming at age 14 in high school. At college I earned a bacholor's degree in electrical engineering; but after working in that field for 2 years, I shifted back to software because it is easy and fun and I’m good at it.

I worked as a software engineer for 30+ years; in regulated and unregulated industries; accruing strength in embedded and communications; doing everything from gathering requirements, to design, coding, unit- and integration-testing, and version control; working in teams and individually, and with various levels of process.

Mostly I learned on the job from other people’s code and a few short courses. Then the world changed and the future was on the web. I took Stanford's early Introduction to Databases MOOC to fill in a gap in my experience, then a couple online courses with Ruby on Rails, git, TDD, BDD, SAAS, MySQL, PostgreSQl, and Java. I have just completed a front-end web development training program.

Now I want to "use my powers for good"— use my software development skills for the healing of the world, for the good of humanity and other living things.

Outside of work, I am an herbalist, a mostly-organic gardener, and a media ecologist, and I enjoy reading cozy mysteries and the occasional sci-fi/​adventure story.
